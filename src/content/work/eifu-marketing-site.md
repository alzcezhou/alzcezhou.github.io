## About the Task

Over several months, I led the end-to-end redesign of the eifu.com marketing website — from information architecture and copywriting through to high-fidelity UI and mobile-responsive layouts. The goal was to modernize the brand's web presence and better communicate the platform's value to prospective customers in regulated industries.

## Research

### Market Research Findings

enLabel's key differentiator is scope & flexibility.

- eIFU offers flexible deployment options that comply with the security needs of varying companies.
- eIFU is built to handle multiple document types across regulated industries.

### Interview with eIFU Sales Director

In this conversation, I identified 3 goals for the marketing website to address:

1. **Diversify Industries** — Each industry has different terminology for their highly-regulated documents. Our product is trademarked as eIFU. How can we ensure visibility of our platform's versatile applications?
2. **Credibility** — Prospective clients need a risk-free way to fully understand eIFU's capabilities and ease of use. We can showcase our clientele on the website with a carousel ribbon, with links to their deployments.
3. **Complexity** — eIFU needs to fit into the documentation workflow of companies with varying compliance needs. The website needs to encourage clients to reach out and easily communicate key information like number of documents, organization size, and budget.

### Pricing

The CEO and eIFU Business teams were key stakeholders in this project. I presented 2 versions of pricing cards for the teams to choose from:
- **Version 1:** Reused pricing cards from eIFU itself, using gradients and simple layouts.
- **Version 2:** Refreshed, modern styling with configurations to emphasize certain plans for strategic business needs.

By presenting a redesign of the pricing cards, I also spurred a visual update for eIFU itself when Version 2 was the clear winner.

## Final Designs

### Landing Page

I aimed to address credibility and complexity with our landing page. The client success stories section features one story at a time, preventing users from feeling overwhelmed by choice. The Features section is designed as Strapi components of Image + Heading + Text with a boolean for left/right rendering, allowing the sales team to configure and swap features to match current market needs.

### Pricing Page

I designed multiple pricing pages to address different stakeholder concerns. The Sales team wanted simple cards with CTAs, while the CEO wanted plans hidden from competitors. I also designed a simple ROI Calculator based on an Excel spreadsheet from the CEO — this interactive component simplifies decision-making for the customer.

### Features & Industries

I designed the features information as a scannable progressive-disclosure system that reveals technical detail upon demand. This helps customers in different roles identify and learn about features relevant to their responsibilities while reducing cognitive load.

The Industries page was designed with a defined content structure prescribed by stakeholders. My contribution was the visual design and layout system.

## Outcome & Reflection

This project was one of the more complex I've navigated professionally. Stakeholders across the company had varying priorities, and my scope shifted page to page.

The designs were not shipped in full. During a transition between internship terms, the company prioritized a faster-to-build solution. As I've moved into a full-time Design & Engineering role, I've been able to bring more of the original work to life incrementally.

My background in Human Factors Engineering shaped how I approached each page — treating them as systems of information rather than visual compositions, applying principles like cognitive load reduction, progressive disclosure, and chunking.

Since completing this project, I've shipped several components from this design system as reusable React components, now live across enLabel's products and marketing sites. Moving forward, I'd invest in the client deployment carousel identified in research, and push for usability testing with real sales prospects.
